## Overview

The AACF Knowledge Document is a **template** that teams fill in to provide the Ticket Writer Gem with the context it needs to generate high-quality, accurate Jira tickets. It is structured into four sections: company background, squad description, relevant context & documentation, and a glossary.

---

## Key Concepts

### Four-Section Structure

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **1. Company / Client Background** | Describes the company, its industry, customers, digital product(s), access model, and constraints |
| **2. Team / Squad Description** | Names the squad, defines their ownership scope, and explicitly calls out what is out of scope |
| **3. Relevant Context & Documentation** | Lists external dependencies, compliance/security/accessibility constraints, and links to PRDs, API specs, and architecture docs |
| **4. Glossary** | Defines all team-specific acronyms and terms (both domain and technical) in a two-column table |

### Completeness Drives Quality
The document explicitly states: *"The more thorough and structured your knowledge document, the more accurate, complete, and actionable the tickets generated by the Gem will be."*

## Important Rules / Constraints

- The document is a **template** — all bracketed placeholders (e.g. `[Insert Squad / Team name]`) must be replaced with actual team information before use.
- The glossary should cover **both** product/domain-specific and technical/internal terms.
- Squad scope must explicitly state what the team does **not** own, to prevent misrouted tickets.
- Compliance, security, accessibility, and localisation requirements belong in Section 3 (Constraints), not the glossary.
